Lindos Acropolis

Lindos Acropolis, situated on the southeastern coast of the island of Rhodes in Greece, is a historic gem that offers a glimpse into the island's ancient past. This archaeological site is a prominent feature in the charming village of Lindos, attracting visitors with its rich history and breathtaking views of the Aegean Sea.

Ancient Architecture: Lindos Acropolis showcases a mix of ancient architectural styles, including Hellenistic, Roman, and Byzantine influences. The site is adorned with impressive structures, including the Doric Temple of Athena Lindia, dating back to the 4th century BCE.

Stoa of Lindos: The Acropolis features the remains of the Stoa of Lindos, a grand portico that once served as a marketplace and meeting place. Its columns and architectural elements reflect the grandeur of ancient Lindos.

Propylaea: Visitors entering Lindos Acropolis pass through the Propylaea, an entrance gateway adorned with intricate carvings and inscriptions, providing a sense of the historical significance of the site.

The Hellenistic Staircase: Ascending Lindos Acropolis involves climbing a Hellenistic staircase, a marvel of ancient engineering. The climb is rewarded with panoramic views of the surrounding landscape and the azure waters of the Mediterranean.

Captivating Views: The Acropolis offers stunning vistas of the Lindos village, its white-washed buildings, and the expansive coastline. The juxtaposition of ancient ruins against the backdrop of the sea creates a scene of unparalleled beauty.
